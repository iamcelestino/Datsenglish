You open the React docs. You read a sentence. You translate it into Portuguese in your head. Then you read it again in Portuguese to actually understand it.

That's two reads for the price of one — and it's costing you more than time.

## The real problem isn't your English

Most tech students think they have an English problem. They don't. They have a **translation habit** problem.

Translating word-for-word forces your brain to process the sentence twice: once to decode the English, once to reconstruct meaning in Portuguese. Native English speakers don't do this. Fluent non-native readers don't do this either. They read the English and understand it *directly* — no detour through their first language.

## Why this matters for your career

Documentation, Stack Overflow answers, GitHub issues, job interviews — almost everything useful in tech lives in English first. If every piece of technical content costs you double the mental effort, you're not just slower. You're avoiding harder material, because your brain unconsciously routes around the extra cognitive load.

That avoidance is the real damage. Not the vocabulary gap — the habit of skipping things that require translation.
